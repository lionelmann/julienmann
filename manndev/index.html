How a Site Gets Built 02 / 04
01
Brief & Discovery
We start with your goals. A 30–60 minute call to understand your audience, what you need the site to do, and what success looks like. I take notes, ask hard questions, and come back with a clear direction. No obligation — if it's not the right fit, I'll say so honestly.
30–60 min call
02
Design
Before a single line of code is written, the site is designed. I establish a visual language — typography, palette, layout — that fits your brand. You see static mockups first. We refine until it's right. Design and development never fight each other when they come from the same person.
Visual identity · Wireframes
03
Build
Clean HTML, CSS, and JavaScript — or a modern stack like Next.js if the project calls for it. Every component is written from scratch, nothing templated. I work in the open: you get a live preview link from day one and can watch the site take shape in real time.
Next.js · TypeScript · Tailwind
04
Review & Refine
Two structured rounds of feedback are built into every project. I test across browsers and devices, optimise for performance, and work through your notes. Lighthouse 100 is the target — not a vanity metric, but a proxy for a site that genuinely loads fast and is accessible to everyone.
Lighthouse · Cross-browser · A11y
05
Launch
Deployment to your domain, analytics wired up, and a walkthrough of how to manage it. You get full ownership of everything — code, hosting, accounts. No lock-in, no monthly platform fees unless you want them. I stay available for 30 days post-launch at no extra charge.
Deployed · Yours to keep
